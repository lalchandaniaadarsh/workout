High Intensity Interval Training + Core Training

Jump Rope

  • Set 1 to Set 10: Skipping for 1 minute & Rest for 30 seconds

Note: Jump rope is an accessible, full-body cardio exercise that offers significant physical and mental benefits, including burning calories and fat quickly, improving cardiovascular health, increasing bone density, enhancing coordination and balance, boosting agility and speed, and sharpening cognitive function and mental sharpness. It's a versatile and fun workout that engages multiple muscle groups and can help reduce stress and improve overall well-being.

High Knees

  • Set 1 to Set 4: High Knees * 50

Note: High knees benefit your overall fitness by improving cardiovascular health, strengthening lower-body and core muscles, and boosting coordination and agility. This versatile exercise burns calories, increases lower-body endurance, and serves as an effective warm-up for more intense workouts.

Jumping Jacks

  • Set 1 to Set 4: Jumping Jack * 50

Note: Jumping jacks provide numerous health benefits, including improving cardiovascular health by strengthening the heart and increasing blood circulation, boosting metabolism and aiding in weight management, and enhancing bone density by being a weight-bearing exercise. They also serve as a convenient full-body workout, toning arms, legs, and core muscles, and can positively impact mood by releasing endorphins.

Shoulder Taps

  • Set 1 to Set 4: Shoulder Tap * 50

Note: Shoulder taps are beneficial for improving core strength and stability, enhancing balance and coordination, increasing shoulder stability and mobility, promoting good posture, and serving as an effective exercise for warming up or adding a full-body challenge to workouts. This exercise engages various muscles, including the abs, obliques, and shoulder stabilizer muscles, to build a stronger, more stable body.

Air Squats

  • Set 1 to Set 4: Air Squat * 50

Note: Air squats, a bodyweight exercise, offer numerous benefits including improved lower body strength, enhanced balance and mobility, and increased calorie burn. They primarily target the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes, while also engaging the core and improving posture. Air squats are a great foundational exercise for more advanced squat variations and can be easily modified for different fitness levels.

Mountain Climbers

  • Set 1 to Set 4: Mountain Climber * 50

Note: Mountain climbers provide a full-body, low-impact workout that strengthens the core, arms, shoulders, glutes, and legs. This exercise boosts cardiovascular endurance, burns calories, and can help reduce body fat while improving balance and posture. By engaging multiple large muscle groups, mountain climbers enhance overall muscle tone and improve functional fitness for everyday activities and sports.

Workout Tips

  • Never perform HIIT right before or after strength training. HIIT causes muscle loss while burning instant calories.
  • Keep HIIT workout session under 20 minutes.
  • Eat low calorie & quick digesting food items before workout. 2 Bananas is a good example.
  • Always ensure performing warmup exercises before starting and cooldown exercises after completing any kind of training.
